# Code of Conduct

FreeAI is a small open-source project. We want issues, pull requests, and
discussions to be a place where anyone can show up and contribute without
having to grow a thick skin first.

## In short

- **Be kind.** Assume good faith. People come from different backgrounds,
  speak different first languages, and have different levels of experience
  with the tools in this repo.
- **Critique code, not people.** "This function is hard to follow" is fine.
  "You're a bad developer" is not.
- **Stay on topic.** Bug reports, feature requests, and design discussion
  are welcome. Politics, personal disputes, and off-topic flame wars are
  not.
- **No harassment.** Targeted insults, slurs, sexual attention you weren't
  asked for, or sharing someone's private information will get you removed.

## If something goes wrong

If a comment or interaction crosses a line, you have two options:

- Open a private [GitHub Security Advisory](../../security/advisories/new)
  — works for community reports too, not just vulnerabilities, and stays
  private until the maintainer decides what to do.
- Or just close the tab and walk away. You don't owe anyone your time.

The maintainer reads everything. If a contributor's behavior is making
the project a worse place to be, that contributor will be asked to stop,
and removed if they don't.

## Scope

This applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other space
where you're representing or interacting with the FreeAI project.
